
= 한국인이 틀리기 쉬운 영어단어
   
  - abandon 
   
   1) abandon은 주로 계획(plan), 활동(activity), 시도(attempt) 등을 포기할 때 쓴다. 
   2) abolish는 법률(law)이나 전통(tradition), 체제(system) 등을 없앨 때는 쓴다.
   
    * Since capital punishment was abandoned, the crime rate has increased.(*)
    * Since capital punishment was abolished, the crime rate has increased.
    
   
  - ability
   
   1) '무엇을 할 능력'이라고 쓰려면 즉 ability 다음에 to 부정사를 쓰지 of -ing를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * These machines are destroying our ability of thinking.(*)
    * These machines are destroying our ability to think.
   
   2) ability 다음에 -ing가 올 경우 of를 쓰고 그 뒤에 -ing를 쓰지 않고 주로 ability앞에 -ing를 쓴다. reading, 
      writing, teaching, acting ability 처럼 쓴다.
   
    * I want to improve my ability of reading.(*)
    * I want to improve my reading ability.
   
   3) 언어나 과목에 대한 능력을 나타낼 때는 of를 쓰지 않고 in을 사용한다.
   
    * I want to improve my ability of English.(*)
    * I want to improve my ability in English.
   
    * She demonstrated considerable ability in both maths and chemistry.
    
   
  - able
   
   1) 무엇을 할 수 있고 실제 행하는 것이 놀랄 만한 것이 아닐 때는 be able to를 쓰고 일반적으로 행하는 것은 
      아니지만 원하면 할 수 있을 때는 be capable of를 쓴다.
   
    * One man is able to destroy the whole world.(*)
    * One man is capable of destroying the whole world.
   
    * The doctor said that after a few days I'd be able to get out of bed.
    * Will you be able to play on Saturday? 
    * I am sure he's quite capable of getting here on time, but he can't be  bothered.
    * The power station is capable of generating enough electricity for the whole region. 
   
   2) see, hear, smell과 같은 감각을 나타내는 동사나 understand, decide, remember과 같은 마음을 나타내는 
      동사의 경우는 be able to 보다는 can이나 can't를 쓴다.
   
    * There are so many places to visit in London that I'm not able to  decide where to go.(*)
    * There are so many places to visit in London that I can't decide where  to go. 
   
    * I can't hear you. Could you speak a bit louder, please?
    * I think I can smell something burning.
    * Having met her new boss, I can see why she doesn't like him.
   
   3) '-하는 것을 멈출 수 없다'의 'can't stop -ing'의 경우는 be able to 로 바꾸어 쓸 수 없다.
   
    * We weren't able to stop laughing.(*)
    * We couldn't stop laughing.
    * The book was so fascinating I couldn't stop reading it. 
   
   4) 법이나 규칙(law, rules) 등으로 규제되는 행동은 can이나 can't 혹은 be allowed to를 쓴다. be able to를 쓰지 
      않는다.
   
    * In some countries you are not able to drink until you are 21. (*)
    * In some countries you can't drink until you are 21.  
   
    * Now that they are both sixteen, they can get married.
    * The goalkeeper can touch the ball, but nobody else can.
   
   5) '누구에게 무엇을 가능하게 하다'라는 뜻을 만들기 위해서는 'make + 목적어 + able to'를 쓰기보다는 'enable 
      + 목적어 + to부정사'를 쓴다.
   
    * Technology has made them able to grow their own food.(*)
    * Technology has enabled them to grow their own food.
    
   
  - about
   
   ♣ 작문시 주의할 점
   
   1) 이야기의 화제(topic)를 도입할 때는 about 보다는 with regard to, regarding, as for, as regards, as far as ___ 
      is concerned 등을 쓴다.
   
    * I am always delighted when I receive a letter from you. About the party on December 26th, 
       I shall be very pleased to attend.(*)
    * I am always delighted when I receive a letter from you. With regard to the party on 
       December 26th, I shall be very pleased to attend. 
   
    * People usually sleep with the doors and windows closed. About public transport, the bus and 
       train services are excellent.(*)
    * People usually sleep with the doors and windows closed. As for public transport, the bus 
       and train services are excellent. 
   
   2) 자신의 개인적인 경험이나 상황을 이야기할 때는 about을 쓰지 않고 as for me, as for myself, in my case를 
      쓴다.
   
    * It all depends on how different the new country is from your own. About myself, I haven't 
       experienced any culture shock but then this is my second trip to the States.(*)
    * It all depends on how different the new country is from your own. In my won case, I 
       haven't experienced any culture shock but then this is my second trip to the States.
   
    * Most of my friends like school. As for me, I can't wait to leave.
   
   3) 줄거리에 대하여 이야기 할 때는 책이나 영화 등에 on을 쓰지 않는다. 전문적이고 심각한 주제에 대하여 할 
      때는 on을 쓴다.
   
   
    
   
  - above
   
   1) 계기의 어떤 점(수치)를 가리킬 경우를 제외하곤 '수가 얼마를 넘는다'라고 할 때는 above를 쓰지 않고 over을 
      쓴다.
   
    * There were above a hundred people in the crowd.(*)
    * There were over a hundred people in the crowd. 
   
    * I receive over twenty letters a day.
    * Don't let the temperature get above thirty degrees. 
   
   2) 우리말에 '무엇보다도'라고 해서 무조건 above all을 쓰면 안되고 '가장 중요한 것은(most importantly)'이라는 
      뜻을 갖는 문장에서 사용해야 한다. 종종 우리말의 '무엇보다도'는 especially가 더 어울릴 경우가 있다.
   
    * He likes reading, above all novels.(*)
    * He likes reading, especially all novels. 
   
    * Get plenty of sleep, eat lots of good food, and above all try to relax.
    * There were many qualities that made him a great leader. Above all, he had charisma. 
   
   3) '다른 어떤 것(사람)보다도(more than anywhere, anything, anyone)'라는 뜻으로 사용될 때는 above all 보다 
      most of all, the most, by far를 쓴다.
   
    * This year English is above all my most important subject.(*)
    * This year English is by far my most important subject. 
   
    * The riot was by far the most horrific scene I'd ever witnessed.
   
   4) above mentioned는 명사 앞에 써야지 명사 뒤에 써서 명사를 수식하지 않는다.
   
    * I would be grateful if you would send it to the address above-mentioned.(*)
    * I would be grateful if you would send it to above-mentioned address.
   
    
   
  - abroad
   
   1) abroad는 부사이기 때문에 전치사(to, in, at)을 붙이지 않는다.
   
    * Since I was small, I've always wanted to go to abroad.(*)
    * Since I was small, I've always wanted to go abroad. 
    * I would like to continue my studies in abroad.(*)
    * I would like to continue my studies abroad. 
   
   2) abroad 앞에 전치사 from은 올 수 있다.
    * She came back from abroad saying how much she had missed her home and family.
    
   
   absent
   
   1) absent는 공식적으로 참가해야 하는 곳에 참가하지 않는 것을 말하고 miss는 그냥 모임에 참가하지 않는 것을 
      나타낸다.
   
    * It's a pity that you were absent from the training session.(*)
    * It's a pity that you weren't at the training session. 
    * It's a pity that you missed at the training session. 
   
    * Her teacher wanted to know why she'd been absent.
    * I wouldn't miss Sandro's party for the world!(=I really want to go to it.)
    * John won't be at the meeting. He's had to fly to Rome on business. 
   
  - accept
   
   1) accept는 충고, 의견, 제안(advice, opinion, suggestion) 등을 받아들일 때 쓴다. 이외의 것에는 agree to를 쓰는 
      것이 좋다.
   
    * The company will not accept to buy new machines.(*)
    * The company will not agree to buy new machines. 
   
    * I accepted her suggestion and agreed to see the doctor that evening.
   
   2) accept는 '목적어가 to부정사하는 것을 받아들이다'라는 식으로 쓸 수 없다. 이럴 때는 allow나 permit를 쓴다.
   
    * The driver did not accept me to get on the bus.(*)
    * The driver did not allowed me to get on the bus. 
   
    * We can't accept a motorway to be built through our town.(*)
    * We can't allow a motorway to be built through our town. 
   
    * Many parents do not allow(permit) their children to watch violent  films.
    * Many parents do not let their children watch violent films. 
   
   3) accept는 to부정사를 목적으로 쓰지 않는다. to부정사를 쓰려면 agree를 쓰는 것이 좋다.
   
    * I accept to do it.(*)
    * I agreed to do it.
   
    
   
  - accident
   
   1) 큰 사고라고 해서 big accident라고 해서는 안되고 bad, dreadful, serious, fatal accident라고 해야한다.
   
    * Her car was involved in a big accident.(*)
    * Her car was involved in a serious accident. 
   
   2) accident는 교통사고처럼 우발적인 사고를 말하고 incident는 계획성이 가미된 사고를 말한다. 종종 좋지 
      않은(unpleasant) 사건을 나타낸다.
   
    * 26 people have been killed in a dramatic shooting incident.
    * The voting went ahead without incident. 
   
   3)  충돌하다고 할 때는 into를 쓴다. with를 쓰지 않는다.
    
    * A car crashed with something.(*)
    * A car crashed into something.
   
    
   
  - accomplish
   
   1) accomplish는 만족(satisfaction)을 주는 어떤 것을 성취하는 경우에 쓰고 이와 유사한 achieve는 계획(plan)했던 
      것이거나 많은 노력을 한 후에 성취한 것에 쓴다. 물론 의미가 중복되는 부분이 있어 서로 구별하기 곤란한 
      경우도 있다.
   
    * To accomplish world unity, we need peace.(*)
    * To achieve world unity, we need peace. 
   
    * A balanced diet is accomplished by eating many different kinds of  food.(*)
    * A balanced diet is achieved by eating many different kinds of food. 
   
    * She felt that she could accomplish more through journalism.
    * During his five years as President, he accomplished very little.
    * The company intends to achieve all these goals within the next five  years.
    * By the age of twenty, she had already achieved her ambition.
    * By the end of the course, you will feel that you've really achieved  something. 
    
   
  - according to
   
   1) according to 다음에는 작가나, 이름, 그들(them) 등이 올 수 있지 자신이 me나 us는 올 수 없다. 따라서 me, 
      us를 쓰려면 in my(our) opinion으로 써야한다.
   
    * According to me, we should spend more money on education.(*)
    * In my opinion, we should spend more money on education. 
   
    
   
  - account
   
   1) account를 'take into account the fact that절' 이라는 식으로 쓸 때 the fact를 생략하면 안 된다.
   
    * We also have to take into account that the schools are overcrowded.(*)
    * We also have to take into account the fact that the schools are overcrowded. 
   
    * They should take into account the fact that these archaeological treasures are extremely 
       valuable.
   
    
   
  - accurate
   
   1)  어떤 글을 쓸 때 기계 등이 정확할 때 쓴다. 시간이나 날짜가 정확하다고 할 때는 exact를 쓴다.
   
    * I cannot give you the accurate date of my arrival yet.(*)
    * I cannot give you the exact date of my arrival yet. 
   
    * Her novels are always historically accurate.
    * These figures can't be accurate, surely.
    * Are you sure the bathroom scales are accurate?
    * The exact time is three minutes to seven.
    * Do you remember his exact words?
    * They'll be arriving next week - on Friday at three, to be exact. 
   
    
   
  - accustom
   
   1) '-에 익숙하다'고 할 때는 be(become, get, grow) accustomed to라고 써야지 그냥 accustom이라고 쓰면 안 
      된다. 그리고 to는 전치사이기 때문에 -ing가 와야지 동사 원형이 와서는 안 된다. 공적인 것이 아닐 때는 
      be(become, get, grow) used to를 써도 된다.
   
    * I am beginning to accustom to the British way of life.(*)
    * I am beginning to get accustomed to the British way of life. 
   
    * Within a few minutes, my eyes had grown accustomed to the dark.
    * I am beginning to get used to the British way of life. 
   
    
   
  - ache
   
   1) ache는 주로 동사로 쓰이거나 아니면 tooth ache, ear ache, head ache, back ache, stomach ache처럼 명사와 
      합쳐 쓰인다. 따라서 I had an ache in my legs. 라고 해서는 안 된다. 굳이 이런 식으로 쓰려면 ache를 쓰지 
      말고 pains를 쓰면 된다.
   
    * After the run, I had an ache in my legs.(*)
    * After the run, my legs were aching.
    * After the run, I had pains in my legs.
    
    * That radio of yours is giving me a headache. 
    * I did some weight training on Monday and my shoulders have been aching ever since.
   
    
   
  - act/action
   
   1) 어떤 구체적인 행동일 때는 act나 behave를 혼용해서 사용해도 된다. 그러나 사람의 일반적인 행동일 때는 
      behave를 써야한다.
   
    * I am interested in the way people act towards each other.(*)
    * I am interested in the way people behave towards each other.
   
    * It's time that human beings learned how to act properly, without killing each other.(*)
    * It's time that human beings learned how to behave properly, without killing each other. 
   
    * You can't expect all babies to behave the same.
    * From the way he behaves, anyone would think that he doesn't get  paid. 
    * Passengers who left the flight in Rhodes said that they had seen two men acting(behaving) 
       very suspiciously.
   
    
   
  - action
   
   1) 동사 do와 action을 같이 쓰지 않는다. do를 쓰려면 do things(something, anything)으로 쓴다.
   
    * The actions that we do everyday are made easier by computers.(*)
    * The things that we do everyday are made easier by computers. 
   
    * If someone has done a wrong action, he should be punished.(*)
    * If someone has done something wrong, he should be punished. 
   
   2) 어떤 구체적인 행동이라는 뜻으로 'an(the) act of (doing) something'이라고 쓸 때는 action 안 쓰고 act를 
      쓴다.
   
    * It is difficult to forgive those responsible for actions of terrorism.(*)
    * It is difficult to forgive those responsible for acts of terrorism. 
   
    * Blackmail is the action of trying to obtain money from someone be threatening to expose 
       them.(*)
    * Blackmail is the act of trying to obtain money from someone be threatening to expose them. 
   
    * For some people, the very act of taling to a doctor can help them to feel better.
   
   3) 때로는 action이 셀 수 없는 명사이므로 관사를 쓰면 안 된다. 특히 '조치를 취하다'라고 할 때는 take 
      action처럼 무관사로 써야한다.
   
    * Few people are aware that an urgent action is needed.(*)
    * Few people are aware that urgent action is needed. 
   
    * The government should take an action to reduce the birth rate.(*)
    * The government should take action to reduce the birth rate. 
   
    * There is too much talk and not enough action.
    * This illegal trade will continue unless action is taken to stop it. 
   
    
   
  - actual
   
   1) actual은 계획된 것이나, 기대되는 것, 믿어지는 것과 반대로 실제로 어떻게 되었다고 할 때 쓴다. 이에 반해 
      지금 발생하거나 존재하는 것(happening, existing)은 present, current를 쓴다.
   
    * We'd like to know more about the actual crisis.(*)
    * We'd like to know more about the present crisis. 
   
    * My actual job involves a lot of administration.(*)
    * My present job involves a lot of administration. 
   
    * People think he is over fifty but his actual age is forty-eight.
    * Although buses are supposed to run every fifteen minutes, the actual waiting time can be 
       up to an hour. 
   
    
   
  - ad(광고)
   
   1) '-에 대한 광고'라고 할 때는 about을 쓰지 않고 for를 쓴다.
   
    * I have just seen your advertisement about a Chinese cook.(*)
    * I have just seen your advertisement for a Chinese cook. 
   
    
   
  - add
   
   1) '무엇을 어디에 첨가하다'라고 할 때는 into를 쓰면 안되고 to를 써야한다.
   
    * The other ingredients are then added into the mixture.(*)
    * The other ingredients are then added to the mixture. 
   
    
   
  - admire
   
   1) admire은 경탄하면서 보는 것을 의미하기 때문에 그냥 단순하게 본다는(look at, see) 의미로 사용해서는 안 
      된다.
   
    * One hour is not long enough to admire all the exhibits.(*)
    * One hour is not long enough to see all the exhibits. 
   
    * I enjoyed admiring all the old buildings.(*)
    * I enjoyed looking at all the old buildings. 
   
   2) 단순히 좋아하다, 즐기다(enjoy)라는 의미로 admire을 사용하면 안 된다.
    * Although it was a sad film, I admired it very much.(*)
    * Although it was a sad film, I enjoyed it very much. 
    
   
   
    
   
  - advantage
   
   1) advantage는 다른 사람보다 나은 위치(a better position)에 있을 때 쓴다. 반면에 merit는 어떤 것의 좋은 
      점이나 강점(a good quality, a strength)을 지칭할 때 사용한다.
   
    * Although the film has its advantages, it also has a serious flaw.(*)
    * Although the film has its merits(good points), it also has a serious flaw. 
   
   2) 현재의 삶이나 상황을 개선할 어떤 이점일 때는 benefit을 쓴다.
   
    * Television provides many advantages.(*)
    * Television provides many benefits. 
   
   3) advantage가 무언가를 하는 이점이라는 뜻으로 the advantage(s)라고 쓸 때는 뒤에 of를 쓴다. 그리고 There is 
      some(several, many) advantage라고 쓸 때는 to나 in을 쓰고 -ing를 쓴다.
   
    * What are the advantages in studying in the United States?(*)
    * What are the advantages of studying in the United States? 
   
    
   
  - advice
   
   1) '충고하다'라는 동사의 뜻은 advice가 아니라 advise이다. advice는 명사로 충고라는 뜻이다.
   
    * I asked my layer for her advise.(*)
    * I asked my layer for her advice. 
   
   2) advice는 셀 수 없는 명사이기 때문에 an을 쓸 수 없고 a piece of를 써야한다. 물론 '여러 충고'라고 할 때는 
      some을 쓰면 된다.
   
    * She gave me a good advice.(*)
    * She gave me some good advice. 
   
    
   
  - advise
   
   1) advise는 목적어로 사람을 쓰고 목적보어로는 to부정사를 쓴다. 만약에 목적어에 사람을 쓰지 않고 생략한다면 
      목적어 자리에 to부정사가 아니라 -ing를 써야한다.
   
    * Nowadays many doctors advise to live in the countryside.(*)
    * Nowadays many doctors advise people to live in the countryside. 
   
   2)  충고하다는 의미로 give advices라고 해서는 안 된다. advice는 셀 수 없는 명사이므로 'give an advice'라고 
      해야 한다. 그리고 some advice라고 해서도 한되고 a piece of advice 혹은 some advice라고 해야 한다.
   
    
   
  - affect
   
   1) affect는 동사이기 때문에 바로 목적어를 쓴다. 반면에 effect는 명사다. effect를 명사로 써서 '-에 대한 
      영향'이라고 할 때는 전치사 on을 쓴다. effect를 동사의 의미로 쓰고 싶을 때는 전치사 on을 취해 have an 
      effect on이라고 쓴다.
   
    * Smoking affects your health.
    * Smoking has an effect on your health. 
   
    * The programme is about computers and their affect on our lives.(*)
    * The programme is about computers and their effect on our lives. 
   
    
   
  - afraid
   
   1) be afraid 다음에 오는 to부정사는 어떤 것이 일어날 것 같아 걱정이 되기 때문에 어떤 것을 하기를 '꺼려할 
      경우'에 쓴다. 반면에 be afraid 다음에 of -ing는 어떤 일이 일어날 것 같아 '걱정할 경우'에 쓴다. 예를 들어 
      기차나 비행기를 놓칠 것 같아 걱정이 되는 경우에는 of -ing가 맞다. 독이든 음식은 먹기를 꺼려한다. 따라서 
      이 경우는 to부정사를 쓰는 것이 옳다.
   
    * The road to the airport was very busy and we were afraid to miss the plane.(*)
    * The road to the airport was very busy and we were afraid of missing the plane. 
    
    * She was afraid to eat it in case it was poisonous.
    * Don't be afraid to ask for help.
    * Most criminals are afraid of being caught.
    * He says that he is afraid of losing his job.
    
   
   
    
   
  - after
   
   1) 지금부터 측정된 미래의 어느 때를 이야기할 때는 in a month's time, in three weeks' time처럼 in을 넣어 
      쓴다. after는 과거에 지나간 일에 대해 쓴다.
   
    * After a week, we're going to Italy.(*)
    * In a week's time we're going to Italy. 
   
    * I hope that I'll still be healthy after ten years.(*)
    * I hope that I'll still be healthy in ten years time. 
   
   2) '후에'라고 말할 때는 after보다는 a month later, three months later처럼 later을 붙여쓴다.
   
    * I promised to meet Hitomi at the exhibition a week after.(*)
    * I promised to meet Hitomi at the exhibition a week later. 
   
    * I returned to Germany after two years' time.(*)
    * I returned to Germany two years later.
   
   3) 완료동사와 함께 쓸 때는 after보다는 since를 써야한다.
   
    * After 1961 the consumption of cheese has increased each year.(*)
    * Since 1961 the consumption of cheese has increased each year. 
   
   4) After가 시간을 나타내는 부사절을 이끌면 after절에는 미래를 쓰면 안되고 현재를 써야한다. (문법에 의하면 
      시간, 때, 조건을 나타내는 부사절에서는 미래 대신에 현재를 쓴다.
   
    * After you will leave, we will write to you every day.(*)
    * After you leave, we will write to you every day. 
   5) after이 부사로 쓰일 수도 있다. 부사로 쓰일 때는 soon after, not long after 등의 구문에서 쓰인다. 단독으로 
      쓰는 부사로 쓰려면 then이나 after that, afterwards 등의 단어를 써야 한다.
   
    * I left college when I was 21, and got married soon after. 
    * We had a game of tennis, and then(after that/afterwards) we went for  a cup of coffee.
   
   6) 한 문장에서 after와 later을 함께 써서는 안 된다.
   
   7) 과거 이야기하고 있을 다음에라고 할 때는 정관사 the를 써서 the next day처럼 써야 하고 말하고 있는 현재 
      이후에라고 할 때는 정관사 the를 쓰지 않고 next day처럼 쓴다.
   
    
   
  - against
   
   1) '어디에 부딪치다'라고 할 때 drive, run, walk, crash 등의 단어를 쓸 때는 into를 써야한다. against를 쓰면 안 
      된다.
   
    * Trying to avoid the sheep, he drove his car against a tree.(*)
    * Trying to avoid the sheep, he drove his car into a tree. 
   
  - age
   
   1) 몇 살이다 할 때는 'be + 나이'로 쓴다.
   
    * I'm at the age of 22) (*)
    * I'm 22 (years old). 
   
   2) '몇 살 된 아이'라고 할 때는 '명사 + 나이' 혹은 '명사 + aged + 나이'라고 쓴다. aged를 생략하게 되면 '명사 
      + 나이 + years of age'로 쓴다.
   
    * A child in the age of seven or eight needs a little push.(*)
    * A child of seven or eight needs a little push. 
   
    * They have two children in the age of 8 and 12) (*)
    * They have two children aged 8 and 12)  
    * They have two children 8 and 12 years of age. 
   
    
   
  - agree
   
   1) 사람이나 의견(opinion)에 같은 생각 즉 동의를 나타날 때는 with를 써서 'agree with + 사람(의견)'으로 쓴다.
   
    * I don't agree the people who say women should stay at home.(*)
    * I don't agree with the people who say women should stay at home. 
   
   2) 무언가를 기꺼이 받아들이거나 허용할 때는 to를 쓴다.
   
    * I don't understand why he doesn't agree the divorce.(*)
    * I don't understand why he doesn't agree to the divorce. 
   
   3) 명사 agreement를 써서 동의한다고 할 때는 make를 안쓰고 reach, come to, work out을 쓴다.
   
    * The government has made an agreement with the People's Republic  of China.(*)
    * The government has reached an agreement with the People's Republic  of China.
   
    
   
  - aid
   
   1) aid는 주로 명사로 쓴다. 따라서 동사로 쓸 때는 aid보다는 help를 쓰는 것이 좋다.
   
    * We must begin to aid ourselves and not wait for other countries.(*)
    * We must begin to help ourselves and not wait for other countries. 
   
   2) '-의 도움으로'라고 할 때는 by가 아니라 with를 써서 'with the aid of'로 나타낸다.
   
    * They can learn faster by the aid of computers.(*)
    * They can learn faster with the aid of computers. 
   
    
   
  - all
   
   1) 셀 수 있는 명사 앞에서 '전부'를 의미할 때는 all 보다는 whole이나 entire를 쓴다.
   
    * This decision changed all of her life.(*)
    * This decision changed whole of her life. 
   
   2) all은 대명사 뒤에 놓여야 하지 앞에 놓여서는 안 된다.
   
    * As all you know, my name is Smith.(*)
    * As you all know, my name is Smith. 
   
    
   
  - alone
   
   1) alone은 그냥 혼자 있는 것이고 lonely는 혼자 있어 외롭다는 뜻을 갖는다.
   
    * I was very alone at first but then I made some friends.(*)
    * I was very lonely at first but then I made some friends.
   
   2) '혼자서' 무슨 일을 했다고 할 때는 alone을 쓰면 안되고(alone은 그냥 혼자 있다는 뜻이기 때문) on one's 
      own을 쓴다.
   
    * Children learn a lot by doing things alone.(*)
    * Children learn a lot by doing things on their own.
   
    
   
  - anger
   
   1) 화를 참다고 할 때는 control one's anger이 아니라 control(keep, lose) one's temper이라고 해야 한다.
   
    * He'll have to learn how to control his anger.(*)
    * He'll have to learn how to control his temper. 
   
    
   
  - answer
   
   1) 문제나 질문(a problem or question)에 대한 답이라고 할 때는 for가 아니라 to를 써야 한다.
   
    * They still can't find an answer for his problem.(*)
    * They still can't find an answer to his problem. 
   
   2) answer이 답장하다, 전화 받다 등 동사로 쓰일 때는 to를 쓰지 않고 바로 목적어를 쓴다.
   
    * It has taken them almost two months to answer to my letter.(*)
    * It has taken them almost two months to answer my letter. 
   
   3) answer의 목적어가 앞에 나온 말을 반복하는 것일 때는 목적어를 쓰지 않고 다른 것일 때만 목적어를 쓴다.
   
    * I rang the number you gave me but nobody answered me.(*)
    * I rang the number you gave me but nobody answered. 
   
    
   
  - apologize
   
   1) '-에 대해 사과하다'고 할 때는 사람을 바로 목적어로 쓰면 안되고 to를 쓰고 사람을 써야한다.
   
    * The waiter was made to apologize my father.(*)
    * The waiter was made to apologize to my father. 
   
   2) '-에 대해 사과하다'고 할 때도 for를 쓰고 대상을 써야한다.
   
    * She insisted on apologizing her husband's behaviour.(*)
    * She insisted on apologizing for her husband's behaviour. 
   
    
   
  - appear
   
   1) 단순하게 '무엇이 일어난다'고 할 때는 '주어 + appear'로 쓰기보다는 'there is '를 쓴다.
   
    * Suddenly, a bright flash of lightning appeared.(*)
    * Suddenly, there was a bright flash of lightning.
   
   2) appear 은 눈에 보이게 무엇이 나타난다는 것을 말하고 보이지 않는 어떤 것이 발생할 때는 occur을 쓴다.
   
    * Unemployment appears in nearly all developed countries.(*)
    * Unemployment occurs in nearly all developed countries. 
   
   3) TV에 나온다고 할 때는 on TV, TV프로그램에 나온다고 할 때는 on(in) TV programme, 영화나 연극에 
      나온다고 할 때는 in을 쓴다.
   
    * If he were alive, he would appear a lot of good films.(*)
    * If he were alive, he would appear in a lot of good films. 
   
    * This is the first time that Britt has appeared on television.
    * He is scheduled to appear on Channel 4 next month. 
   
    
   
  - apply
   
   1) 직업이나 연수과정 (job, course, scholarship) 등에 지원할 때는 for를 쓰고 조직에(organization)에 지원할 때는 
      to를 쓴다.
   
    * I've applied a one-year course in computer studies.(*)
    * I've applied for a one-year course in computer studies. 
   
   2) 지원하는 것이 아니라 '-를 대상으로 한다(be aimed at)'고 할 때는 to를 쓴다.
   
    * The new tax law applies only on people with large incomes.(*)
    * The new tax law applies only to people with large incomes. 
   
    
   
  - approach
   
   1) '-에 접근하다'고 할 때는 대상 앞에 to를 쓰지 않고 바로 대상을 쓴다.
   
    * He approached to my table and took a seat opposite me.(*)
    * He approached my table and took a seat opposite me. 
   
    
   
  - appropriate
   
   1) appropriate는 어떤 구체적인 상황에 적확하다는 뜻이고 relevant는 행하거나 이야기하는 것과 어떤 관련이 
      있을 때 쓴다.
   
    * The third paragraph of the essay is not appropriate.(*)
    * The third paragraph of the essay is not relevant.
   
    * Will you please send me all the appropriate information?(*)
    * Will you please send me all the relevant information?
   
   2) 필요한 자질이나 기술(qualities, skills)을 갖고 있어 적당할 때는 suitable을 쓴다. appropriate나 suitable은 
      의미가 유하사기 때문에 서로 바꾸어 사용할 수 있는 경우가 있다.
   
    * Some bottles are not appropriate for recycling.(*)
    * Some bottles are not suitable for recycling. 
   
    
   
  - argument
   
   1) argument는 특히 상대를 설득하기 위해 하는 자신의 주장을 말하며 reason은 말 그대로 어떤 행동이나 느낌 
      등에 대한 이유를 나타낸다.
   
    * There are several good arguments for people preferring to live in the  countryside.(*)
    * There are several good reasons for people preferring to live in the  countryside. 
   
   2) 동사 argue는 어떤 것에 대해 강하게 동의하지 않을 때 쓴다. 종종 화나는 일이 종종 있을 경우에 쓴다. 
      반면에 의견이 달라 여러 관점에서 이야기하게 될 때는 have a discussion을 쓴다.
   
    * In the first class after lunch we usually argue.(*)
    * In the first class after lunch we usually have a discussion.
   
    
   
  - arise
   
   1) arise는 happen과 같은 자동사다. 따라서 수동태로 쓰지는 않는다. 반면에 arouse는 타동사다. 따라서 '-이 
      일어나다'라고 할 때는 수동태로 써야한다.
   
    * These problems have been arised as a result of overpopulation.(*)
    * These problems have arisen as a result of overpopulation. 
   
    
   
  - arrive
   
   1) '어떤 도시에 도착하다'고 할 때는 in을 쓰고 역이나 공항, 건물(station, airport, building)에 도착할 때는 at을 
      쓴다.
   
    * We'll arrive to London at two in the morning.(*)
    * We'll arrive in London at two in the morning. 
   
    * She arrived the station just in time to catch the train.(*)
    * She arrived at the station just in time to catch the train. 
   
    
   
  - artificial
   
   1) artificial은 자연적인 것이 아니라 인공적인 것으로 만들어진 것(not made of natural materials)을 말하며 
      man-made는 사람에 의해 만들어진 것을 말한다.
   
    * The city has suffered both natural and artificial disasters.(*)
    * The city has suffered both natural and man-made disasters. 
   
   2) 어떤 화학적 작용에 의해 만들어진 인위적인 것에는 synthetic을 쓴다.
   
    * He managed to lose weight by eating artificial diet foods.(*)
    * He managed to lose weight by eating synthetic diet foods. 
   
    
   
  - as
   
   1) 명사를 비교할 때 '-같은'의 뜻은 as가 아니라 like를 쓴다. as는 as-as, not so-as, the same - as에서 사용한다.
   
    * His skin is not as the skin of a young man.(*)
    * His skin is not like the skin of a young man. 
   
   2) 어떤 범주(class, category)를 이야기할 때 '-같은'이라고 할 때는 such as나 like를 쓴다.(as를 쓰지 않는다)
   
    * The book is concerned with important social issues as child abuse and women's rights.(*)
    * The book is concerned with important social issues such as child abuse and women's rights.
   
   3) 실제 어떤 상황이 아닌데 '-인 것처럼'할 때는 as if(가정법)를 쓴다.
   
    * She looked at everyone as she were their superior.(*)
    * She looked at everyone as if she were their superior. 
   
   4) as well as 다음에는 -ing를 쓴다.
   
    * Every week he wrote her three letters, as well as telephoned her.(*)
    * Every week he wrote her three letters, as well as telephoning her. 
   
    
   
  - ashamed
   
   1) 나쁜 어떤 것을 했기 때문에 부끄러워하는 것은 ashamed이고 사회적 통념으로 편안치 못하고 걱정이 될 때는 
      embarrassed를 쓴다.
   
    * I always feel ashamed when I have to speak in public.(*)
    * I always feel embarrassed when I have to speak in public. 
   
    
   
  - aspect
   
   1) aspect는 어떤 상황, 생각, 문제 등의 특징이나 그 일부 양상을 의미하며 point of view는 어떤 상황, 사건, 
      문제 등을 바라보는 특별한 관점을 말한다.
   
    * From a biological aspect, the two plants are very similar.(*)
    * From a biological point of view, the two plants are very similar. 
   
   2)  이미 말한 어떤 것을 되짚어 이야기 할 때 '이러한 점에서'라고 표현하려면 in this respect라고 해야한다. in 
      this aspect라고 하지 않는다.
   
    * Another function of newspapers is to shape public opinion and in this aspect the Guardian 
       is very influential indeed.(*)
    * Another function of newspapers is to shape public opinion and in this respect the Guardian 
       is very influential indeed. 
   
    
   
  - assure
   
   1) '확신시키다'라고 할 때는 'assure + 사람 + that'이라고 해야하며 'assure that'이라고는 하지 않는다.
   
    * I can assure that your name will not be mentioned.(*)
    * I can assure you that your name will not be mentioned. 
   
    
   
  - attention
   
   1) 위험하거나 해로울 수 있는 어떤 상황에 주의한다고 할 때는 pay attention to가 아니라 be careful, take 
      care이라고 한다.
   
    * Everyone should pay attention to avoiding stressful situations.(*)
    * Everyone should take care to avoid stressful situations. 
   
   2) 많이 주의를 집중하다고 할 때는 full이나 undivided attention이라고 쓴다.
   
    * When driving, you must give your best attention to what you are doing.(*)
    * When driving, you must give your full attention to what you are doing. 
   
    
   
  - audience
   
   1) 교회에 모인 사람들은 congregation이고 어떤 집회나, 영화관, 콘서트에 모인 사람들은 audience이다.
   
    * The priest stands in front of the bride and groom, facing the audience.(*)
    * The priest stands in front of the bride and groom, facing the congregation.
   
    
   
  - awake
   
   1) awake는 주로 깨어있는 이라는 형용사로 사용되며 동사로 '일어나다'라고 할 때는 wake up이라고 쓴다.
   
    * I get up at seven o'clock but I awake an hour earlier.(*)
    * I get up at seven o'clock but I wake up an hour earlier. 
   
    
   
  - award
   
   1) award는 무엇인가를 잘해서 주어지는 상, 자격증, 메달(prize, certificate, medal) 등을 말하며 reward는 
      누군가에게 준다고 한 상금이나 누군가를 기쁘게 해줌으로써 주어지는 보답을 말한다.
   
    * The insurance company has offered an award to anyone who can provide information about 
       the theft.(*) 
    * The insurance company has offered an reward to anyone who can provide information about 
       the theft. 
   
   
    
    
   
  - badly 
   
   1) badly는 be pp나 have pp 사이에 쓴다.
   
    * My shoulders were sunburnt badly.(*)
    * My shoulders were badly sunburnt.
   
    
   
  - base
   
   1) base는 어떤 것의 주된 부분을 말하고 basis는 어떤 것이 발전(발달하거나 만들어질 수 있는 바탕이 되는 매우

    * Political stability provides the base for economic development.(*)
    * Political stability provides the basis for economic development. 
   
    
   
  - bear
   
   1) bear는 애기를 낳는 물리적인 행위를 말한다. 따라서 bear의 주어는 언제나 여자다. 반면에 가족 중 아이의

    * The government also controls the number of children that a couple  can bear.(*)
    * The government also controls the number of children that a couple  can have. 
   
   2) '참을 수 없다'고 할 때는 can't bear it처럼 반드시 it을 써야한다.
   
    * 'I can't bear any longer', he said. 'I'm ready to resign.'(*)
    * 'I can't bear it any longer', he said. 'I'm ready to resign.' 
   
    
   
  - bed
   
   1) 침대에서 일어났다고 해서 get up from my bed라고 하지 않고 그냥 get up이라고 한다. get up 자체에 이미 
      침대에서 일어났다는 말을 포함하고 있다.
   
    * Every morning I have to get up from my bed very early.(*)
    * Every morning I have to get up very early.(*) 
   
   2) 잠자리에 들다. 일어나다 등의 표현 stay in bed, go to bed, get out of bed, lie in bed, be in bed 등에는

    * I decided to stay in my bed this morning.(*)
    * I decided to stay in bed this morning. 
   
    
   
  - before
   
   1) ago는 말하는 순간인 지금부터 전이라는 뜻이고 before는 과거의 어느 때 그 이전(before 'then' a time in the

    * Before forty years, the journey took twice as long.(*)
    * Forty years ago, the journey took twice as long. 
   
    
   
  - begin

    * At the beginning of the century people travelled from Britain to Egypt  by sea.

    * In those days, he began studying law.
   
    
   
  - believe
   
   1) believe in은 종교와 같은 어떤 믿음, 생각으로서 어떤 것을 믿는다는 말이고 believe는 객관적으로 사실에 대해

    * You should not believe in everything you hear.(*)
    * You should not believe everythit hardly believe in my eyes.(*)
    * I can't hardly believe my eyes.

    * I deeply believe that tourist will stop coming here.(*)
    * I firmly believe that tourist will stop coming here. 
   
    
   
  - below
   
   1) below는 어떤 것보다 낮은 곳에 있는 것을 말하며 under는 덮혀있거나 접촉해 있는 어떤 것 바로 아래를

    * Below the table there were some empty bottles.(*)
    * Under the table there were some empty bottles. 
   
   2) 어떤 글에서 아래에 있는 어떤 것이라고 쓸 때는 명사 뒤에 below를 써야한다. 명사 앞에 쓰면 안 된다.

    * The below report describes my recent stay at the Hotel Grove.(*)
    * The report below describes my recent stay at the Hotel Grove. 
   
   3) 비율이 훨씬 낮다고 할 경우에는 far below를 쓰며 much는 쓰지 않는다. 반면에 lo첨가하다'라는 뜻)의 뜻이다.

    * Besides this problem, her husband had lost his job. 
   
    
   
  - blame

   1) broken은 물질적으로 손해, 손상을 입었을 때 쓰고 no working은 기능이 제대로 작동하지 않을 때 쓴다.

    * I went back into the house to fetch my sunglasses.

    * The play deals with the struggle of a married couple to live their own lives. 
   
   3) '다루다'라고 해서 다 deal with를 쓰면 안되고(2번 참고) 특별한 기술을 요하는 장비를 다룬다고 할 때는 
      use(operate, handle)을 쓴다.
   
    * These days even young schoolchildren know how to deal with computers.(*)
    * These days even young schoolchildren know how to use computers. 
   
    
   
  - degree
   
   1) 학위라는 뜻으로 쓸 때는 the가 아니라 a를 쓴다.
   
    * I have the degree in economics.(*)
    * I have a degree in economics. 
   
   2) 어떤 분야의 학위라고 할 때는 분야 앞에 in을 쓴다.
   
    * He is studying for a degree of computer science.(*)
    * He is studying for a degree in computer science. 
   
    
   
  - demand
   
   1) 법률이나 규칙 때문에 어떤 것을 해야할 경우는 require을 쓴다.
   
    * In my new job I am demanded to work overtime.(*)
    In 무엇에 대한 요구'라고 할 때는 a(the) demand for를 쓴다.
   
    * The demand of butter has decreased in recent years.(*)
    * The demand for butter has decreased in recent years. 
   
    
   
  - deny
   
   1) '사실(true)이 아니다'고 말할 때는 deny를 쓰고 '앞으로 무엇을 하지 않겠다'고 말할 때는 refuse를 쓴다.
   
    * He asked his parents to help him, but they denied.(*)
    * He asked his parents to help him, but they refused. 
   
   2) deny는 타동사이므로 반드시 목적어를 써야한다.
   
    * She accused him of cheating but he denied.(*)
    * She accused him of cheating but he denied it.
   
   
   
   
  - depend
   
   1) It depends on 다음에는 if는 올 수 없고 whether이 올 수 있다.
   
    * It depends on if you've got enough money.(*)
    * It depends on whether you've got enough money. 
   
    
   
  - describe
   
   1) '누구에게 무엇을 묘사한다'고 할 때는 '대상 + to 사람'으로 쓴다. '사람 + 대상'으로는 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * He described me his sister in great detail.(*)
    * He described his sister to me in great detail. 
   
   2) '-에 대한 묘사'라고 할 때는 about을 쓰지 않고 of를 쓴다. description of로 쓴다.
   
    * They needed a description about the stolen car.(*)
    * They needed a description of the stolen car. 
   
    
   
  - despite
   
   1) despite는 전치사이므로 절(주어+동사)이 올 수 없다. 절을 쓰려면 although를 써야한다.
   
    * Despite the train was empty, he came and sat in front of me.(*)
    * Although the train was empty, he came and sat in front of me. 
   
   2) despite는 of를 쓰지 않고 단독으로 쓴다. 반면에 spite는 of를 써서 in spite of로 쓴다.
   
    * Despite of my qualifications, I couldn't get a job.(*)
    * Despite my qualifications, I couldn't get a job. 
   
    
   
  - destroy
   
   1) 즐거움이나 기쁨을 없앨 때는 spoil이나 ruin을 쓴다.
   
    * This unpleasant man destroyed my journey.(*)
    * This unpleasant man spoilt my journey. 
   
   2) 쓸모 없게 되거나 덜 효율적이 되었을 때도 spoil이나 ruin을 쓴다.
   
    * The water had been much too hot and most of the clothes were destroyed.(*)
    * The water had been much too hot and most of the clothes were ruined. 
   
    
   
  - difficulty
   
   1) '어려움을 겪다'고 할 때는 관사(a)를 쓰지 않고 'have difficulty(difficulties) in -ing'로 쓴다. to부정사를 쓰지 
      않는다.
   
    * She had a difficulty in obtaining a visa.(*)
    * She had difficulty (in) obtaining a visa. 
   
    
   
  - disagree
   
   1) '같은 의견이 아닐 때'는 disagree를 쓰고, '하라는 것을 하기 싫을 때'는 refuse를 쓴다.
   
    * Those who disagreed to join the army were put in prison.(*)
    * Those who refused to join the army were put in prison. 
   
   2) '동의하지 않는다'고 할 때는 with를 쓴다. to를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * She disagreed totally to what I said.(*)
    * She disagreed totally with what I said.(*) 
   
    
   
  - disappear
   
   1) 자동사이기 때문에 수동태로 쓰지 않는다. 그리고 사라졌다는 의미는 완료를 의미하므로 완료의 형태로 잘 
      쓰인다.
   
    * The next morning, the snowman was disappeared.(*)
    * The next morning, the snowman disappeared. 
   
    
   
  - discuss
   
   1) 타동사이기 때문에 전치사(about, on)을 쓰지 않고 바로 목적어를 쓴다.
   
    * They'd like to discuss about what to do next.(*)
    * They'd like to discuss what to do next. 
   
   2) discussion은 말 그대로 이야기를 주고 받는 토론을 의미하고 argument는 논쟁이나 불일치(a quarrel or 
      disagreement)를 말한다.
   
    * He ran away from home after a discussion with his father.(*)
    * He ran away from home after an argument with his father. 
   
    
   
  - distance
   
   1) 두 장소(건물)간에 멀리 떨어진 거리를 이야기할 때는 a long distance가 아니라 a long way를 쓴다.
   
    * It was a long distance between the hotel and the beach.(*)
    * It was a long way from the hotel to the beach. 
   
   2) 두 장소나 건물이 아니라 그냥 먼 거리 할 때는 distance를 쓴다.
   
    * I hate driving long distances.
    * Trains are excellent for long distance travel. 
   
   3) '어떤 장소로부터 몇 마일 떨어져 있다'고 할 때는 away를 쓴다.
   
    * The nearest town is in a distance of ten miles.(*)
    * The nearest town is ten miles away.
   
    
   
  - district
   
   1) district는 어떤 도시의 공식적인 구역을 말한다. 그리고 '부자동네(a wealthy district)처럼 특정한 특징이 있는 
      도시 지역을 말할 때도 쓴다. 반면에 region은 일반적으로 공식적인 경계가 없는 넓은 지역을 말한다. 어떤 
      것을 써야할지 모를 때는 area를 쓰면 무난하다.
   
    * We will be touring the famous fruit-growing districts.(*)
    * We will be touring the famous fruit-growing regions. 
   
    * the South Cambridgeshire District
    * the Liverpool 8 district of Merseyside
    * an old slum district where the gangs operate
    
   
   
  - doubt
   
   1) '무엇을 의심하다'고 할 때는 주로 'be in doubt about'이라고 쓴다.
   
    * Whenever I doubt about the meaning of a word, I look in my dictionary.(*)
    * Whenever I am in doubt about the meaning of a word, I look in my dictionary. 
   
   2) 그리고 doubt를 명사로 써서 '의심하다'는 뜻으로 쓸 때는 have doubt about이라고 쓴다.
   
    * That is why we still doubt beings existing in outer space.(*)
    * That is why we still have doubts about beings existing in outer space. 
   
   3) that절을 취한다고 해서 'it is no doubt that'이라 하지 않고 'There is no doubt that'이라고 쓴다.
   
    * It is no doubt that the rich have a great advantage.(*)
    * There is no doubt that the rich have a great advantage. 
   
   4) without doubt는 사실이라고 확실히 믿는다는 뜻이고 no doubt는 추측된다, 예상된다(expect, suppose)정도의 
      뜻으로 쓴다.
   
    * Without doubt you're tired after your journey.(*)
    * No doubt you're tired after your journey.
   
    
   
  - downstairs
   
   1) downstairs는 주로 부사로 쓰이기 때문에 전치사(to, in, at)을 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * I ran to downstairs and picked up the telephone.(*)
    * I ran downstairs and picked up the telephone. 
   
    
   
  - drunken
   
   1) drunken은 사람을 묘사할 때는 잘 쓰지 않고 사람들의 행동(drunken driving)을 묘사할 때 잘 쓰인다. 그리고 
      명사 앞에 쓴다. 단독으로(서술적으로) 쓸 때는 drunk를 쓴다. 술 취한 사람은 a drunk이다.
   
    * The man was obviously drunken.(*)
    * The man was obviously drunk. 
   
    * One of the two drunken men shouted at me.(*)
    * One of the two drunks shouted at me. 
   
    
   
  - during
   
   1) during이나 for 둘 다 '-동안'이라는 뜻이지만 구체적인 숫자가 올 때는 for, 사건이 올 때는 during을 쓴다. 
      during은 'when', for는 'How long?'의 질문에 대한 답으로 생각하면 된다.
   
    * After the accident, I had to stay in hospital during three months.(*)
    * After the accident, I had to stay in hospital for three months. 
   
   2) '평생동안'이라고 해서 during all my life하지 않고 그냥 'all my life'라고 쓴다.
   
    * My uncle has known me during all my life.(*)
    * My uncle has known me all my life. 
   
   
   
   
  - each 
   
   1) each 다음에는 단수명사를 쓴다.
   
    * We had to answer each questions on a new page.(*)
    * We had to answer each question on a new page. 
   
   2) 'each of 복수명사'가 주어 자리에 오면 실제 주어는 each이므로 단수동사를 써야한다.
   
    * Each of the nurses were very kind.(*)
    * Each of the nurses was very kind. 
   
   3) each는 인칭대명사 뒤에 쓴다.
   
    * They have each their own problems.(*)
    * They each have their own problems. 
   
    
   
  - earn
   
   1) earn은 일해서 돈을 벌 때 쓰며 win은 복권 등에서 운좋게 돈을 벌 경우에 쓴다.
   
    * He earned a lot of money on the lottery.(*)
    * He won a lot of money on the lottery. 
   
    
   
  - earnest
   
   1) earnest는 삶(life)을 너무 심각하고 진지하게 생각해서 종종 삶을 유머스럽게 보지 못할 때 쓴다. 이와 
      유사하게 serious는 무언가에 완전히 몰두해 있는 사람을 묘사할 때 쓰는 말이다.
   
    * The earnest students never missed a class.(*)
    * The serious students never missed a class. 
   
    
   
  - earth
   
   1) earth는 우주에서 본 지구, 물리학적 측면에서 본 지구, world는 사람, 나라, 도시 등의 관점에서 본 지구, 
      planet은 자연환경(natural environment)의 관점에서 본 지구라고 말할 때 쓴다.
   
    * I have friends in different parts of the earth.(*)
    * I have friends in different parts of the world. 
   
    * We must all work together to protect the earth.(*)
    * We must all work together to protect the planet. 
   
   2) '지구에서' 할 때는 in을 쓰면 안되고 on을 쓴다.
   
    * The aliens were fascinated by life in earth.(*)
    * The aliens were fascinated by life on earth. 
   
    
   
  - easy
   
   1) use, make, read 등의 동사와 더불어 '쉽게 무엇을 할 수 있다'고 쓸 때는 주로 be easy to use(make, read, 
      use) 등의 형식으로 쓴다.
   
    * These computers can be used easily.(*)
    * These computers are easy to use.
   
   2) easy는 사람을 주어로 해서 잘 표현하지 않는다. 주로 'it is easy for 사람 to do'로 쓴다.(진주어 가주어 구문)
   
    * In London you easily get from one place to another.(*)
    * In London it's easy to get from one place to another. 
   
   3) 2번과 유사하게 가목적어, 진목적어 구문으로도 잘 쓴다.
   
    * The next morning I didn't get up easily.(*)
    * The next morning I found it difficult to get up.
    * The next morning I had difficulty (in) getting up. 
   
    
   
  - effect
   
   1) '영향을 미치다'라는 동사의 의미로 쓸 때는 have an effect on을 쓴다. 아니면 동사 affect를 써도 된다. 
      effect는 명사이다.
   
    * I thought that the long illness would effect my chances of passing the exam.(*)
    * I thought that the long illness would affect my chances of passing the exam. 
   
   2) '-에 미치는 영향'이라고 할 때는 on을 쓴다. to를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * Many women stop smoking during pregnancy because of the effects to the baby.(*)
    * Many women stop smoking during pregnancy because of the effects on the baby. 
   
   3) 형용사 effective를 써서 '어디어디에 효과적이다'라고 할 때는 to부정사를 쓰지 않고 in을 써서 be effective in 
      -ing 형태로 쓴다.
   
    * Aspirin is very effective to relieve pain.(*)
    * Aspirin is very effective in relieving pain. 
   
    
   
  - efficient
   
   1) efficient는 어떤 일이 빨리 그리고 낭비 없이 (quickly and without waste)진행될 때 쓰며 effective는 바라는 
      결과(영향; desired effect)을 까져올 때 쓴다.
   
    * The medicine proved very efficient.(*)
    * The medicine proved very effective.
   
    
   
  - else
   
   1) '-을 제외한'이란 의미로 쓸 때는 else than처럼 else와 than을 같이 쓰지 않는다. other than을 주로 쓴다.
   
    * She said that she hadn't told anybody else than her husband.(*)
    * She said that she hadn't told anybody other than her husband. 
   
    
   
  - end
   
   1) '마침내' 할 때는 in the end로 쓴다.
   
    * At the end I decided not to go.(*)
    * In the end I decided not to go. 
   
   2) at the end는 뒤에 of를 취해서 '-의 끝에'라는 뜻으로 쓴다.
   
    * In the end of the dinner someone made a speech.(*)
    * At the end of the dinner someone made a speech. 
   
   3) in the end는 어떤 것의 결과(the result or outcome of something)를 소개할 때 쓴다. finally는 오랜 시간 후나 
      지연이 된 후에 어떤 일이 발생했을 때 쓴다.
   
    * In the end, I would like to wish you all a very interesting and enjoyable stay.(*)
    * Finally, I would like to wish you all a very interesting and enjoyable stay. 
   
   4) '-이 끝나다'라는 뜻의 자동사로 쓰인다. 수동태로 쓰면 안 된다.
   
    * The film was ended at eighty-thirty.(*)
    * The film ended at eighty-thirty. 
   
    
   
  - endure
   
   1) 고통스럽거나 유쾌하지 못한 상황을 견딜 때는 endure을 쓰고 병이나 병적인 것을 겪을 때는 suffer from을 
      쓴다.
   
    * More than 160 million people endure malaria.(*)
    * More than 160 million people suffer from malaria. 
   
    
   
  - enjoy
   
   1) enjoy는 타동사이므로 목적어를 반드시 써야한다.
   
    * During the Christmas holidays I really enjoyed.(*) 
    * During the Christmas holidays I really enjoyed myself.
   
   2) enjoy는 -ing를 목적어로 쓴다. to부정사를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * I enjoy to speak foreign languages.(*)
    * I enjoy speaking foreign languages. 
   
    
   
  - enough
   
   1) 주어가 충분하다(주어 + be enough)식으로 잘 쓰지 않고 'there is(are)'로 쓴다.
   
    * Time isn't enough for us to catch the next train.(*)
    * There isn't enough time for us to catch the next train. 
   
   2) enough는 부사이지만 형용사 뒤에서 앞에오는 형용사를 수식한다. 그리고 주로 뒤에는 to부정사가 온다.
   
    
   
  - enter
   
   1) enter는 타동사이므로 전치사(into)없이 바로 목적어를 쓴다.
   
    * The number of foreign workers that are allowed to enter into Japan has increased.(*)
    * The number of foreign workers that are allowed to enter Japan has increased. 
   
   2) 기차, 비행기, 배를 탈 때는 get on the board로 쓴다.
   
    * I entered the train in Oslo.(*)
    * I got on the train in Oslo. 
    I boarded the train in Oslo. 
   
   3) '대학에 들어가다'고 할 때는 get into를 쓴다.
    * Unfortunately, some of my classmates didn't enter university.(*)
    * Unfortunately, some of my classmates didn't get into university. 
   
    
   
  - especially
   
   1) especially는 문장의 첫 머리에 올 수 없고 주어 뒤에 쓴다.
   
    * In the south of Germany there is a different mentality. Especially the Bavarians are very 
       lively and cheerful.(*)
    * In the south of Germany there is a different mentality. The Bavarians especially are very 
       lively and cheerful. 
   
    
   
  - establish
   
   1) 비교적 오래 운영할 큰 회사나 조직(company, organization)을 설립할 때는 establish를 쓰고 가게나 
      레스토랑(shop, restaurant)을 개점할 때는 open을 쓴다.
   
    * I want to establish a cooperative store.(*)
    * I want to open a cooperative store. 
   
    
   
  - etc
   
   1) and etc. 처럼 and와 etc는 같이 쓰지 않는다. 그냥 컴마를 찍고 etc.를 쓰면 된다.
   
    * They make televisions, radios, and etc.(*)
    * They make televisions, radios, etc. 
   
    
   
  - ever
   
   1) ever은 완료시제와 같이 쓰여 경험의 뜻을 나타낸다. ever에는 '전에(before)'라는 뜻이 이미 들어가 있기 
      때문에 before와 같이 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * She was the most unpleasant person that I had ever met before.(*)
    * She was the most unpleasant person that I had ever met. 
   
    
   
  - every
   
   1) every 다음에는 단수명사가 온다.
   
    * There was a representative from every countries.(*)
    * There was a representative from every country. 
   
   2) 매일, 매주는 every day, every week로 쓸 수 있지만 '며칠마다'라고 할 때는 every two days처럼 복수로 
      쓴다.
   
    * I have to visit the hospital every six week.(*)
    * I have to visit the hospital every six weeks. 
   
    
   
  - example
   
   1) '-의 예'라고 할 때는 for를 쓰지 않고 of를 쓴다.
   
    * Medicine is a good example for something that we pass from one generation to the next.(*)
    * Medicine is a good example of something that we pass from one generation to the next. 
   
   2) 본보기가 되다라고 할 때는 set을 쓴다. give를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * A manager is supposed to give a good example.(*)
    * A manager is supposed to set a good example. 
   
    
   
  - except
   
   1) except는 '포함되지 않는다'는 것을 의미하고 besides는 '첨가한다'는 의미다.
   
    * Except smoke and traffic fumes, there are several other types of pollution.(*)
    * Besides smoke and traffic fumes, there are several other types of pollution. 
   
   2) except는 '-을 제외하고'라는 전치사이지만 뒤에 -ing를 쓰지 않고 to부정사를 쓴다.
   
    * I had never really spoken to him except saying hello.(*)
    * I had never really spoken to him except to say hello. 
   
    
   
  - excuse
   
   1) excuse me는 
   1) 누군가의 말을 가로챌 때, 2) 누구 앞을 지나갈 때, 3) 모르는 것을 누구에게 물어 볼 때 쓴다. 사과할 때는 I'm 
      sorry를 쓴다.
   
    * Excuse me. I didn't know that you wanted to speak to me.(*)
    * I'm sorry. I didn't know that you wanted to speak to me. 
   
   2) excuse는 비교적 작은 잘못이나 위반일 때 쓰고 forgive는 작거나 크거나 간에 모든 잘못에 쓸 수 있다.
   
    * He was sorry that he had lied to her and asked her to excuse him.(*)
    * He was sorry that he had lied to her and asked her to forgive him. 
   
    
   
  - exercise
   
   1) '운동하다'고 할 때는 make를 쓰지 않고 do를 쓴다.
   
    * Make sure that you eat properly and don't forget to make you exercises.(*)
    * Make sure that you eat properly and don't forget to do you exercises. 
   
    
   
  - expand
   
   1) 크기, 지역, 활동, 사업(size, area, activity)등이 크게 될 때는 expand를 쓰고, 수, 양, 비용(number, amount, 
      cost)이 커지면 increase를 쓴다.
   
    * I'm doing the course to expand my job opportunities.(*)
    * I'm doing the course to increase my job opportunities. 
   
   2) 질병, 불, 사회적 문제, 느낌(disease, fire, social problem, feeling) 등이 퍼질 때는 spread를 쓴다.

      형태로 사용해야 한다. 그리고 의미상의 차이를 보면, get 보다는 have가 좀더 소극적인 사역(명령)을 나타내는 
      것이 보통이다.

      may have got to go.라고 하지는 않는다. 또 must의 뜻을 갖는 have to는 must 대신에 미래형, 과거형, 
      미래완료, 완료형, 진행형에 사용한다. 그리고 앞뒤의 문맥에 따라 to 이후의 부분을 생략할 수 있다. 예를 
      들어, Do you have to go out now? - I have to (go out now) 등으로 사용할 수 있다.
   
   3) -해야한다는 have to의 부정은 '-할 필요가 없다'는 don't have to로 쓴다. have not to로 쓰지 않는다.

    * He was pleased that he didn't have to look after the baby.

   2) '-에 대한 필요'라고 할 때는 for를 쓴다. of를 쓰지 않는다. for 뒤에는 명사가 온다. -ing를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * There is a great need of international understanding.(*)
    * There is a great need for international understanding. 
   
   3) need 다음에 동사를 쓰려면 to부정사를 써야한다. for -ing나 of -ing를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * For a long time I have felt the need of improving my French.(*)
    * For a long time I have felt the need to improve my French. 
   
   4) '-할 필요가 없다'고 할 때는 need not과 don't have to를 쓸 수 있다. 지금(now)의 상황을 언급할 때는 need 
      not을 쓰고 일반적인 상황을 언급할 때는 don't have to를 쓴다.
   
    * During that day I need not work.(*)
    * During that day I don't have to work. 
   
    * You needn't rush. I'm not in a hurry. 
   
    
   
  - nervous
   
   1) nervous는 자신감이 없어서 불안한 것을 말하고 anxious는 뭔가가 일어날 것 같아 걱정하는 것을 말한다.
   
    * Thinking she might be hurt, I felt very nervous.(*)
    * Thinking she might be hurt, I felt very anxious.
   
   2) irritate는 반복적이고 지속적인 어떤 것으로 사람을 괴롭히거나 화나게 만들 때 쓴다.
   
    * What really made me nervous was the way he kept pulling my sleeve.(*)
    * What really irritated me nervous was the way he kept pulling my sleeve. 
   
    
   
  - next
   
   1) 과거에서 다음에 할 때는 정관사를 써서 the next day라고 쓴다. 반면에 현재 이후에 할 때는 정관사 the를 
      쓰지 않고 next day처럼 쓴다.
   
    
   
  - noise
   
   1) noise는 의미가 없으며 불쾌한 소리를 말하고 sound는 귀에 들 리는 의미를 알 수 있는 소리를 말한다.
    
    * I turned on the radio but there was no noise.(*)
    * I turned on the radio but there was no sound. 
   
   2) '큰 소리' 할 때는 big을 쓰지 않고 loud를 쓴다.
   
    * I was waken up by a big noise outside the room.(*)
    * I was waken up by a loud noise outside the room. 
   
    
   
  - notice
   
   1) notice는 정보나 지시사항(information or direction)을 적은 것을 말하고 note는 다른 사람에게 전해주는 짧은 
      글을 말한다.
   
    * I wrote him a notice saying that the package had arrived.(*)
    * I wrote him a note saying that the package had arrived. 
   
   2) notice는 can이나 could와 함께 쓸 수 없다. 같이 쓰려면 see를 쓴다.
   
    * I could notice that he was not enjoying himself.(*)
    * I could see that he was not enjoying himself. 
    * I noticed that he was not enjoying himself.
   
    
   
  - nowadays
   
   1) '요즘' 할 때 nowadays는 부사이므로 전치사(in)을 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * People live longer in nowadays.(*)
    * People live longer nowadays. 
   
   2) 부사이므로 형용사처럼 쓸 수 없다.
   
    * This is a major problem for nowadays society.(*)
    * This is a major problem for today's society.
   
    
   
  - number
   
   1) the number of는 '-의 수'라는 뜻이므로 단수 동사를 쓴다.
   
    * The number of heavy smokers are decreasing.(*)
    * The number of heavy smokers is decreasing. 
   
   2) a number of는 '많은'이라는 뜻이므로 복수 동사를 쓴다.
   
    * A large number of cars was parked outside the school.(*)
    * A large number of cars were parked outside the school. 
   
   3) 셀 수 없는 명사에는 number을 쓰면 안되고 amount를 쓴다.
    
    * Harsher punishments will not reduce the number of crime.(*) 
    * Harsher punishments will not reduce the amount of crime. 
   
    
    
   
  -  obey 
   
   1) '에게 복종하다'고 해서 to를 쓰지 않는다. 바로 목적어를 쓴다.
   
    * He was a good boy and obeyed to his parents all the time.(*)
    * He was a good boy and obeyed his parents all the time. 
   
    
   
  - object
   
   1) object는 행동이나 행사(action, event)의 목적(purpose)을 말하고 objective는 앞으로 하려고 하는 것이나 어떤 
      행위의 끝에 가서 획득하기를 원하는 것을 말한다.
   
    * My object is to improve my English as much as possible.(*)
    * My objective is to improve my English as much as possible. 
   
    
   
  - obligation
   
   1) obligation은 도덕적 의무나 책임감을 말하고 duty는 직업의 일부로서 해야하는 일 중의 하나이기 때문에, 혹은 
      옳다고 생각하기 때문에 해야하는 것을 말한다.
   
    * My obligations include doing the housework and picking up the children from school.(*)
    * My duties include doing the housework and picking up the children from school. 
   
    
   
  - oblige
   
   1) force나 compel은 상대방이 하기 싫은 것을 하도록 시키는 것이다.(이 경우 사역동사 make를 쓸 수 있다)
   
    * You can't oblige children to study if they don't want to.(*)
    * You can't force children to study if they don't want to. 
   
    
   
  - obtain
   
   1) 무엇인가를 얻는데 많은 시간이 걸리고 많은 노력이 들 때는 achieve를 쓴다.
   
    * It has taken women a long time to obtain equality.(*)
    * It has taken women a long time to achieve equality. 
   
   2) obtain은 주로 격식있는 문장(formal style)에서 쓰고 일반적인 문장에서는 '얻다'라는 의미로는 get을 쓴다.
   
    * Where did you obtain the ticket?
    * Where did you get the ticket?
   
    * How long does it take to get a visa? 
   
    
   
  - occur
   
   1) occur은 일반적으로 계획되지 않은 일(unplanned event)이 발생했을 때 쓴다. 계획된 일이 일어날 때는 take 
      place를 쓴다.
   
    * The concert will occur at eight o'clock next Tuesday.(*)
    * The concert will take place at eight o'clock next Tuesday. 
   
   2) occur은 격식 있는 문장에서 주로 사용되며 일반적인 문장에서는 happen을 쓴다.
   
    * You'd better tell them exactly what occurred.(*)
    * You'd better tell them exactly what happened. 
   
    
   
  - of
   
   1) 날짜를 이야기할 때 12월 25일을 말로 하면 'the 25th of December'이나 'December the 25th'라고 한다. 그러나 
      글로 쓸 때는 'December 25th'나 '25th December'이라고 한다. 글로 쓸 때는 the나 of를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * I arrived in London on 25th of November.(*)
    * I arrived in London on 25th November. 
   
    
   
  - old
   
   1) 나이를 나타낼 때 be동사 뒤에는 나이를 쓰고 years old를 쓴다.
   
    * She is eight years old.
    * He is married to a twenty years old American girl.(*)
    * He is married to a twenty-year-old American girl. 
   
   2) 나이를 나타낼 때 of를 쓰면 나이만 쓰면 된다. '명사 + of + 숫자'.
   
    * He fell in love with a young girl of nineteen years old.(*)
    * He fell in love with a young girl of nineteen.
   
    
   
  - one
   
   1) '-중에 하나'로 one of를 쓸 때 of 뒤에는 복수명사를 쓴다.
   
    * The sea is one of our main source of food.(*)
    * The sea is one of our main sources of food. 
   
   2) one of가 주어가 되면 단수동사를 쓴다.
   
    * One of the eggs were bad.(*)
    * One of the eggs was bad. 
   
   3) 명사의 반복을 피하기 위해 one을 쓰나 소유격(-'s) 뒤에는 one을 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * After we had been to Helen's house, we went to Paul's one.(*)
    * After we had been to Helen's house, we went to Paul's. 
   
   4) these나 those 바로 뒤에 ones를 쓰는 것은 피한다. these나 those 뒤에 형용사를 쓰면 ones을 써도 좋다.
   
    * If you can carry those books, I'll bring these ones.(*)
    * If you can carry those books, I'll bring these.
   
    * These plastic ones are cheaper.
   
    
   
  - opinion
   
   1) '-에 따르면'이라고 할 때는 according to를 쓴다. in one's opinion으로 해도 좋다. 그러나 according to one's 
      opinion이라고는 하지 않는다.
   
    * According to Henry's opinion, less money should be spent on weapons.(*)
    * According to Henry, less money should be spent on weapons. 
   
   2) 의견(opinion)을 나타낼 때는 express나 give를 쓴다. say를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * They are not afraid of saying their opinions.(*)
    * They are not afraid of expressing their opinions. 
   
    
   
  - opportunity
   
   1) 무엇인가가 일어날 것 같은 것에 대해 이야기 할 때는 chance를 쓴다.
   
    * There is an opportunity that David's father will come tonight.(*)
    * There is a chance that David's father will come tonight.
   
    
   
  - opposite
   
   1) '-맞은 편에'라고 할 때는 opposite 다음에 to나 of를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * On the wall opposite to the door, there was a large painting.(*)
    * On the wall opposite the door, there was a large painting. 
   
   2) 이야기되고 있는 사람(사물)의 맞은 편이라고 할 때는 그 명사(사람) 뒤에 opposite를 쓴다.
   
    * The opposite woman was knitting a cardigan.(*)
    * The woman opposite was knitting a cardigan. 
   
    
   
  - order
   
   1) 명령할 만한 위치에 있기 때문에 무엇을 하라고 시킬 때는 order을 쓴다. 그런 위치에 있지 않은 경우라면 
      ask를 써야한다.
   
    * I was so excited that I forgot to order them to check that the camera was working 
       properly.(*)
    * I was so excited that I forgot to ask them to check that the camera was working properly.
    
   
   
    
   
  - originate
   
   1) originate는 풍습이나 사상, 물건 등이 어디에서 기원한다고 이야기 할 때 쓴다. 사람이 어디서 태어나고 
      성장했다고 할 때는 come from을 쓴다.
   
    * One of our teachers originates from Scotland.(*)
    * One of our teachers comes from Scotland. 
   
   2) 용어(언어)가 어디에서 기원했다고 할 때는 come from이나 be derived from을 쓴다.
   
    * 'Alcohol' originates in Arabic.(*)
    * 'Alcohol' comes from Arabic. 
   
    
   
  - otherwise
   
   1) otherwise는 '그렇지 않다면'의 뜻을 가진 부사이다. 따라서 or처럼 절을 이끌지 못한다. 명령문 뒤에 or을 쓰게 
      되면 or의 뜻은 '그렇지 않다면'이다.
   
    * Remember to get there early otherwise you may not get a seat.(*)
    * Remember to get there early. Otherwise you may not get a seat.
    * Remember to get there early or you may not get a seat. 
   
    
   
  - outside
   
   1) '-에서 나가다'라고 할 때는 get(go) out of를 쓴다. outside를 쓰지 않는다. outside는 '밖에서'라는 뜻이다.
   
    * The new airport makes it easy to go outside the country.(*)
    * The new airport makes it easy to go out of the country. 
   
    
   
  - overdue
   
   1) 돈을 지불한 날자, 도서관에서 빌린 책의 반납기간, 비디오 대여기간 등이 지난 경우에는 overdue를 쓰고 
      증명서, 계약, 회원 등의 기간이 지난 경우에는 expire를 쓴다.
   
    * I suddenly realized that my driving licence was overdue.(*)
    * I suddenly realized that my driving licence was expired. 
   
    
   
   
   
  - painful 
   
   1) painful은 '고통을 주는'이라는 뜻이므로 '고통을 느끼다'라고 할 때는 feel painful이라고 하면 안 된다. feel 
      pain이라고 해야한다.
   
    * The operation was successful but I still feel very painful.(*)
    * The operation was successful but I still feel a lot of pain.
   
    
   
  - pair
   
   1) pair은 양말처럼 두 개가 한 짝을 이루는 것을 말한다. 그리고 늘 함께 다니는 한 쌍의 동물을 말하기도 한다. 
      반면에 남편과 아내를 말할 때는 pair라고 하지 않고 couple이라고 한다.
   
    * The old pair next door have been married for 65 years.(*)
    * The old couple next door have been married for 65 years.
   
    
   
  - paper
   
   1) paper은 셀 수 없는 명사이기 때문에 a를 붙이지 못하며 무엇인가를 Tm는 구체적인 종이 한 장 할 때는 a 
      sheet of를 쓴다.
   
    * Each of us was given a clean paper to write on.(*)
    * Each of us was given a clean sheet of paper to write on. 
   
    
   
  - pardon
   
   1) 'I beg your pardon'은 우연히 다른 사람과 부딪쳤을 때나 실수로 남의 자리에 앉았을 때 쓴다. 그리고 
      상대방의 말을 제대로 알아듣지 못했을 때도 쓴다. 그 외의 경우에는 'I'm sorry'를 쓴다.
   
    * I asked the teacher if I could be pardoned for a few minutes.(*)
    * I asked the teacher if I could be excused for a few minutes. 
   
    * I beg your pardon, but I was very busy and couldn't spare the time.(*)
    * I'm sorry, but I was very busy and couldn't spare the time. 
   
    * I'd like to beg your pardon because I was late.(*)
    * I'm sorry I was late. 
    * Please forgive me for being late. 
   
    
   
  - part
   
   1) part of 앞에는 a를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * A part of the difficulty was caused by her poor English.(*)
    * Part of the difficulty was caused by her poor English. 
   
   2) '대부분'할 때는 most part of로 하지 않고 most of로 한다. 그러나 for the most part라고는 쓸 수 있다. 
      이것은 굳어진 표현이다.
   
    * The country is vast and occupies the most part of the continent.(*)
    * The country is vast and occupies most of the continent. 
   
   3) party나 wedding에 참석한다고 할 때는 go를 쓴다. take part in을 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * Did you take part in her party on Saturday?(*)
    * Did you go to her party on Saturday? 
   
   4) club이나 society(사교모임)에 참석할 때는 join을 쓴다.
   
    * I think you ought to take part in a club.(*)
    * I think you ought to join a club. 
   
    
   
  - partly
   
   1) partly가 '어느 정도, 조금'이라는 뜻으로 쓰일 때 문장 끝에는 쓰지 않는다. 문장 끝에서 이러한 의미로 쓸 
      때는 to some extent, so some degree를 쓴다.
   
    * Putting fluoride in the water may solve the problem partly.(*)
    * Putting fluoride in the water may solve the problem to some extent.
   
    
   
  - party
   
   1) '파티을 열다'고 할 때는 have, hold, throw를 쓴다. make나 celebrate는 쓰지 않는다. celebrate는 Christmas, 
      the New Year, birthday, retirement(은퇴), promotion(승진) 등에 쓴다.
   
    * On christmas Day we always make a big party.(*)
    * On christmas Day we always have a big party. 
   
    * The party was being made at a friend's house.(*)
    * The party was being held at a friend's house. 
   
    
   
  - pass
   
   1) pass는 휴일이나 시간을 보낼 때 쓴다. 시간을 보낸다는 의미로 pass를 쓰지 않는다. pass는 week, month, 
      year 등의 시간을 주어로 해서 시간이 흐른다는 의미의 자동사로 쓴다.
   
    * We like to pass our holidays near the sea.(*)
    * We like to spend our holidays near the sea. 
   
    * Two weeks passed and there was still no reply.
   
   2) 시간을 나타낼 때는 비인칭 주어 it을 쓴다. 하지만 pass는 it을 주어로 해서 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * It has passed almost a year since we first met each other.(*)
    * It's almost a year since we first met each other. 
   
    
   
  - pattern
   
   1) 매일 매일 행하는 일상적인 것을 말할 때는 routine을 쓴다.
   
    * Their daily pattern doesn't include enough exercise.(*)
    * Their daily routine doesn't include enough exercise. 
   
    
   
  - pay
   
   1) pay는 '얼마를 지불하다'라는 의미다. 따라서 pay의 목적어로는 금액이 와야하며 물건이 올 때는 for를 
      써야한다.
   
    * Who paid the tickets?(*)
    * Who paid for the tickets? 
   
   2) pay가 명사로 쓰일 때는 셀 수 없는 명사이므로 a를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * Very few office workers get a good pay.(*)
    * Very few office workers get good pay. 
   
   3) '돈을 돌려준다'고 할 때: 빌린 돈을 갚을 때는 pay back을 쓰고 샀는 물건을 돈으로 환불 할 때는 refund를 
      쓴다.
   
    * The manager offered to pay me back the cost of the camera.(*)
    * The manager offered to refund the cost of the camera. 
   
   4) 의사나 변호사 및 다른 전문직종의 사람에게 돈을 지불하는 것은 fee라 한다.
   
    * Dr. Kim charged a high payment but he is very good.(*)
    * Dr. Kim charged a high fee but he is very good. 
   
    
   
  - perform
   
   1) '영화나 연극에서 어떤 특정한 역할을 한다'고 할 때는 play를 쓴다. perform은 특정한 역할이 아니고 그냥 
      '공연하다'라는 일반적 의미로 쓰인다.
   
    * In his last film he performed a middle-aged school teacher.(*)
    * In his last film he played a middle-aged school teacher. 
   
    
   
  - period
   
   1) '시간이 걸리다'라고 할 때는 take a long(short) time처럼 time을 쓰지 period를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * These traditions took a long period of time to evolve.(*)
    * These traditions took a long time to evolve. 
   
    
   
  - permission
   
   1) permission은 셀 수 없는 명사이다. 따라서 a를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * To get a job in Korea, foreigners need a special permission.(*)
    * To get a job in Korea, foreigners need special permission. 
   
   2) permission 앞에는 the를 단독으로 쓰지 않는다. the farmer's permission처럼 소유격을 쓸 때 the를 쓸 수 
      있다.
   
    * Eventually he gave me the permission to stay at home.(*)
    * Eventually he gave me (his) permission to stay at home. 
   
    
   
  - person
   
   1) all(every) people 이라고 하지 않으며 대신 everybody, everyone이라고 쓴다.
   
    * Very soon every person will have a portable computer.(*)
    * Very soon everyone will have a portable computer. 
   
   2) people은 가 자체가 복수(사람들)이다. 그러나 a를 쓰게 되면 '하나의 민족'이라는 뜻이 된다. 물론 
      복수(peoples)가 되면 '민족들'이라는 뜻이다.
   
    
   
  - place
   
   1) place는 어떤 특정 장소를 말하고 room은 '빈 공간'을 말한다.
   
    * I hope there's enough place in the wardrobe for all your clothes.(*)
    * I hope there's enough room in the wardrobe for all your clothes. 
   
    
   
  - pleasant
   
   1) pleasant는 사람이 '예의바르고 다정한(polite, friendly)'이라는 뜻이고 pleased는 '만족한, 행복한(satisfied, 
      happy)라는 의미다.
   
    * We would be very pleasant if you could attend.(*)
    * We would be very pleased if you could attend. 
   
    
   
  - plural
   
   1) 보통 복수명사를 사용하는 것이라도 명사 + 명사에는 단수형
    a trouser press, a billiard table, shoe shop, 
    an eight-foot bear, a ten-dollar bill
    2) 복수 그대로 쓰는 경우
    a glasses case 안경집 arms race 군비 확장경쟁
    custom office 세관 customers guidance 직업안내
    watches and clothes 시계류와 의류코너
   
    
   
  - possibility
   
   1) '-한 가능성'이라고 할 때는 of -ing를 쓴다. to부정사를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * We are considering the possibility to do the job ourselves.(*)

    * Let us hope that there will be a peaceful solution of these problems.(*)
    * Let us hope that there will be a peaceful solution to these problems. 
   
    
   
  - somebody
   
   1) somebody는 평서문에서 쓰고 anybody는 부정문이나 의문문에 사용한다. someone도 마찬가지다. 부정문, 
      의문문에서는 anyone을 쓴다.
   
    * She had not seen somebody for over a week.(*)
    * She had not seen anybody for over a week. 
   
    
   
  - something
   
   1) something도 someone과 마찬가지다. 평서문에서는 something을 쓰고 부정문, 의문문에서는 anything을 쓴다.
   
    * At first we couldn't see something suspicious.(*)
    * At first we couldn't see anything suspicious. 
   
    
   
  - sorry
   
   1) '-에 대해 미안하다'고 할 때 전치사는 about이나 for를 쓴다. about 뒤에는 명사나 -ing를 쓰고 for 뒤에는 
      -ing를 쓴다.
   
    * I'm sorry for my terrible handwriting.(*)
    * I'm sorry about my terrible handwriting. 
   
    
   
  - space
   
   1) 우주를 의미할 때는 the를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * There are hundreds of millions of stars in the space.(*)
    * There are hundreds of millions of stars in space. 
   
    
   
  - speak
   
   1) 한 사람이 전부 혹은 거의 대부분 이야기 할 때 speak를 쓴다. '-에게 이야기 할 때' 혹은 '이야기를 걸 때'도 
      speak를 쓴다. 둘 이상의 사람들이 대화를 나눌 때는 talk를 쓴다. 어느 나라의 말을 할 때는 speak를 쓴다.
   
    * We hadn't seen each other for a year and so we spent the whole night speaking.(*)
    * We hadn't seen each other for a year and so we spent the whole night talking. 
   
    
   
  - speed
   
   1) '어떤 속도로'하면 at을 써서 at high(great, full) speed라고 쓴다. with나 in을 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * These new trains can travel in very high speeds.(*)
    * These new trains can travel at very high speeds. 
   
    
   
  - spend
   
   1) '돈이나 시간을 -하느라고 쓰다'라고 할 때는 on을 쓴다. for를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * They don't have any money to spend for luxuries.(*)
    * They don't have any money to spend on luxuries. 
   
   2) '-하는데 시간을 쓰다'라고 표현할 때는 -ing를 쓴다.
   
    * Parents should spend more time to look after their children.(*)
    * Parents should spend more time looking after their children.(*) 
   
    
   
  - sport
   
   1) sport 그 자체는 셀 수 없는 명사다. 그래서 일반적인 의미의 스포츠라고 할 때는 복수형(sports)을 쓰지 
      않는다.
   
    * Sports help us to keep fit.(*)
    * Sport helps us to keep fit. 
   
   2) 명사 앞에 sport를 쓸 때는 구체적인 sport를 의미하므로 복수형(sports)로 쓴다.
   
    * I need some new sport shoes.(*)
    * I need some new sports shoes. 
   
    
   
  - steal
   
   1) 은행이나 우체국 등이 도둑맞았다고 할 때는 rob을 쓴다.
   
    * The bank in our town has been stolen twice this year.(*)
    * The bank in our town has been robbed twice this year. 
   
   2) 사람이 주어가 되어 도둑 맞았을 때도 rob을 쓴다. steal을 쓰지 않는다. steal은 사람을 목적어로 취하지 
      않으므로 사람을 주어로 해서 수동태를파업하다'라고 할 때는 go(be) on strike라고 쓴다. strike 앞에 a를 쓰지 
      않는다.
   
    * The whole workforce is threatening to go on a strike.(*)
    * The whole workforce is threatening to go on strike. 
   
    
   
  - suggest
   
   1) suggest는 that 절을 목적어로 취한다.
   
    * I suggest you to take more exercise.(*)
    * I suggest you (that) you take more exercise. 
   
   2) suggest는 -ing를 목적어로 쓴다. to부정사를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * She suggested to go to the zoo.(*)
    * She suggested going to the zoo. 
   
    
    
   
  - talk 
   
   1) 어떤 나라의 언어를 말할 때는 speak를 쓴다.
   
    * I couldn't talk English in those days.(*)
    * I couldn't speak English in those days.
   
   2) 사람을 목적어로 취할 때는 tell을 쓴다. tell은 'tell + 사람 + about + 사물' 혹은 'tell + 사람 + to부정사', 'tell 
      + 사람 + that절' 등으로 쓰인다.
   
    * I'd like to talk you about a dream I had last night.(*)
    * I'd like to tell you about a dream I had last night.(*) 
   
   3) talk가 사람을 목적어로 쓰려면 to나 with를 쓴다.
   
    * There's something I'd like to talk you about.(*)
    * There's something I'd like to talk to you about. 
   
    
   
  - taste
   
   1) taste는 어떤 맛인지 알아보기 위해 음식을 입에 조금 넣고 맛보는 것이고 try는 처음으로 어떤 음식을 맛볼 때 
      쓴다.
   
    * During your visit you'll be able to taste some of the local specialities.(*)
    * During your visit you'll be able to try some of the local specialities.(*) 
   
   2) have good(poor, no) taste처럼 '어떤 취향을 갖는다'고 할 때는 a를 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * I buy all my family's clothes because my husband has such a poor taste.(*)
    * I buy all my family's clothes because my husband has such poor taste. 
   
   3) '어떤 취향이다'를 be동사를 써서 표현하려면 in을 써서 'be in good(poor, bad) taste'라고 쓴다.
   
    * We all thought that the joke was of a very bad taste.(*)
    * We all thought that the joke was in very bad taste. 
   
    
   
  - television
   
   1) 일반적으로 TV라고 할 때의 TV는 셀 수 없는 명사를 말한다. 하지만 구체적인 물건으로서의 TV를 일컬을 
      때는 셀 수 있는 명사이기 때문에 a나 복수형을 쓸 수 있다.
   
    * The whole world watched the cup final on televisions.(*)
    * The whole world watched the cup final on television. 
   
    Some parents buy two televisions - one for the children to watch and one for themselves.
   
   2) 'TV에서 방영되는'라고 할 때는 on을 쓴다.
   
    * Cigarettes are no longer advertised in television.(*)
    * Cigarettes are no longer advertised on television. 
   
    
  - tell
   
   1) tell을 목적어로 사람을 쓰고 직접목적어로 that절을 쓴다. 'tell + 사람 + that절'
   
    * He told that he hadn't eaten anything for over a week.(*)
    * He told them that he hadn't eaten anything for over a week. 
    * He said that he hadn't eaten anything for over a week. 
   
   2) tell을 사물을 목적어로 취할 수 없다. 사물을 목적어로 쓸 때는 say를 쓴다.
   
    * John said that he was trying to get to London. 'That's where I'm going," told the man.(*)
    * John said that he was trying to get to London. 'That's where I'm going," said the man. 
   
   3) tell은 'tell + 사람 + about'으로 쓸 수 있다. 사람을 생략하면 talk를 쓴다.
    'talk about'으로 쓴다.
   
    * He was always telling about herself and her problems.(*)
    * He was always talking about herself and her problems.(*) 
   
    
   
  - thank
   
   1) 감사하는 뜻인 thankful이나 grateful을 엄격하게 구별할 수는 없지만 어떤 좋은 일이 일어나서 '안심하거나 
      즐거운(happy and relieved)' 이라는 뜻을 가질 때 thankful을 쓴다. grateful은 감사하는 마음이 가득한(full of 
      thanks)이라는 뜻이다.
   
    * I'm very thankful to you for giving me this opportunity.(*)
    * I'm very grateful to you for giving me this opportunity. 
   
    
   
  - therefore
   
   1) therefore는 부사이지 접속사가 아니다. 따라서 절을 단독으로 이끌 수 없다.
    * She has lived in New York for many years therefore she regards New York as her home.(*)
    * She has lived in New York for many years. Therefore she regards New York as her home.
   
    
   
  - think
   
   1) 예의 바른 초대나 요구를 할 때는 관용적으로 'I was wondering if(whether)'을 쓴다.
   
    * I was thinking if you would like to have lunch before visiting the museum.(*)
    * I was wondering if you would like to have lunch before visiting the museum. 
   
   2) 깊이 생각한다고 해서 deeply를 쓰지 않고 seriously나 carefully을 쓴다.
   
    * He advised me to think deeply about it before making a decision.(*)
    * He advised me to think seriously about it before making a decision. 
   
   3) think가 전달동사로 쓰이고 that절이 올 때: 이러한 문장의 부정은 think를 주로 부정하지 that절 이하에서 
      부정하지 않는다. 다시 말해 'don't think that'문장을 주로 사용하여 'think that - not'이라고는 잘 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * I think she didn't understand what you said.(*)
    * I don't think she understood what you said. 
   
    
   
  - throw
   
   1) 어떤 대상을 때리려는 의도로 던질 때는 at을 쓴다.
   
    * He picked up a small stone and threw it to Sally's window.(*)
    * He picked up a small stone and threw it at Sally's window. 
   
    
   
  - time
   
   1) '오랜 시간'할 때는 관사를 써서 a long time이라 한다.
   
    * The journey takes long time.(*)
    * The journey takes a long time. 
   
   2) all the time은 '계속해서, 줄곧'이라는 뜻이고 every time은 '매번'이라는 뜻이다.
   
    * Medical science is developing every time.(*)
    * Medical science is developing all the time. 
   
   3) '좋은 시간을 보낸다'고 할 때는 have a good time을 쓰지 spend을 쓰지 않는다.
   
    * We spent a very good time in New York.(*)
    * We had a very good time in New York. 
   
    
   
  - title
   
   1) 책, 연극, 그림, 영화, 음악의 제목은 title이고 신문기사의 제목은 headline이다.
   
    * The newspaper titles were all about the earthquake.(*)
    * The newspaper headlines were all about the earthquake. 
   
   2) 책, 연극, 그림, 영화 등을 일컬을 때는 be called 혹은 be entitled를 쓴다.
   
    * He was reading a short story by Saki titled 'The Open Window'.(*)
    * He was reading a short story by Saki called 'The Open Window'. 
   
    
   
  - too
   
   1) too는 합리적이거나 받아들일 만한 것 혹은 좋은 것 이상인'이라는 뜻이다. very의 대용으로 무조건 쓸 수는 
      없다.
   
    * I was too happy when I received you letter today.(*)
    * I was very happy when I received you letter today. 
   
   2) 부정문에서 '역시, 또한'할 때는 either을 쓴다. too는 긍정문에서 쓴다.
   
    * For some reason they didn't receive the fax and your letter hasn't arrived too.(*)
    * For some reason they didn't receive the fax and your letter hasn't arrived either.
    
   
   
  - top
   
   1) '-의 꼭대기에, 상단에'할 때는 at을 써서 at the top(bottom)을 쓴다.
   
    * You will find my address on top of the letter.(*)
    * You will find my address at the top of the letter. 
   
    
   
  - tremble
   
   1) 무섭거나 흥분해서 몸을 떨 때는 tremble을 쓰고 추워서 몸을 떨 때는 shiver을 쓴다.
   
    * It was so cold that I couldn't stop trembling.(*)
    * It was so cold that I couldn't stop shivering. 
    
   
    
  - understand 
   
   1) '깊이 이해한다'고 할 때는 fully, really를 쓴다. deeply를 쓰지 않는다. fully(really) understand라고 쓴다. 잘 
      이해한다고 할 때는 better(properly)를 쓴다.
   
    * You have to know our culture in order to understand us deeply.(*)
    * You have to know our culture in order to really understand us.
   
    * Nobody fully understands how the drug actually works.
   
    
   
  - uniform
   
   1)  군인, 경찰, 간호원 등이 근무할 때 입는 옷은 uniform이고 배우 등이 공연할 때 입는 옷은 custom이다.
   
    * Every year we make new uniforms for the carnival.(*)
    * Every year we make new customs for the carnival.
   
    
   
  - until
   
   1) until이나 till은 시간과 관계해서 쓰며 장소와 관계해서는 쓰지 않는다. 장소와 관계해서 '-까지'는 to를 쓴다.
   
    * The new tunnel means that we can drive until London.(*)
    * The new tunnel means that we can drive to London.
   
   2) '특정 나이까지' 할 때는 up to를 쓴다.
   
    * The school caters for children until the age of eleven.(*)
    * The school caters for children up to the age of eleven.
   
   3) 특정 나이까지 어떤 행동이나 상태가 지속될 때는 until을 쓰고 지속의 상태가 아니라 완료의 상태를 나타낼 
      때는 by를 쓴다.
   
    * I will have finished this book until tomorrow.(*)
    * I will have finished this book by tomorrow.
   
   4) 아마 계속될 것 같은 어떤 행동에 대해 '지금까지'라고 할 때는 so far를 쓴다.
   
    * Until now we have received over sixty applications.(*)
    * So far we have received over sixty applications.
   
   5) 말하고 있는 순간이나 어떤 행동이 말하는 순간까지 일어나지 않은 것을 나타낼 때는 yet을 쓴다.
   
    * The letter hasn't arrived until now.(*)
    * The letter hasn't arrived yet.
   
    
   
  - use
   
   1) 현재의 습관은 현재형으로 쓰고 과거의 습관은 'used to 동사원형'을 쓴다.
   
    * In my previous job I use to travel a lot.(*)
    * In my previous job I used to travel a lot.

    * In my previous job I use to travel a lot.(*)
    * In my previous job I used to travel a lot.
   
    
    
   
  -  wait 
   
   1) wait는 누군가 혹은 무엇이 올 때까지 어느 장소에서 기다리는 행동을 말하고 expect는 누군가 혹은 무엇이 올 
      것이라고 믿는 생각을 말한다.
   
    * I'm waiting a letter from my boyfriend.(*)
    * I'm expecting a letter from my boyfriend.
   
   2) 기다린다고 할 때는 for를 반드시 써야한다.
   
    * I stopped and waited the truck to pass.(*)
    * I stopped and waited for the truck to pass.
   
    
   
  - wash
   
   1) '자신의 몸을 씻는다'고 표현할 때는 주로 have a wash(bath, shower)를 쓴다.
   
    * I wash my body and then get dressed.(*)
    * I have a bath(shower) and then get dressed.
   
    
   
  - way
   
   1) '-하는 방법'이라는 뜻으로 way를 쓸 때 describe, explain, examine 등의 동사가 오면 way를 쓰지 않고 how를 
      쓴다.
   
    * He explained the ways how we can help to protect the sea.(*)
    * He explained how we can help to protect the sea.
   
    
   
  - weekend
   
   1) '주말에'라고 할 때는 on 이나 during을 쓰지 않고 at을 써서 at the weekend라고 한다.
   
    * I seldom stay at home on weekend.(*)
    * I seldom stay at home at the weekend.
   
    
   
  - welcome
   
   1) welcome은 동사뿐만 아니라 형용사로도 쓰인다. 동사로서의 welcome은 손님이나 방문객을 환영할 때 쓰며 
      형용사로서의 welcome은 파티나 집에 손님이 오게 돼서 기쁠 때 쓴다.
   
    * You can bring as many friends to the party as you like. Everyone is welcomed.(*)
    * You can bring as many friends to the party as you like. Everyone is welcome.
   
    
   
  - when
   
   1) 두 가지 일이 거의 동시에 일어날 때는 when을 쓰지 않고 on -ing를 쓴다.
   
    * When hearing that the child had been found, she burst into class.(*)
    * On hearing that the child had been found, she burst into class.
   
   2) when이 시간을 나타내는 부사절일 경우에는 when절에는 미래를 쓰지 않고 현재를 쓴다.
   
    * When I'll return home, I'll look for a better job.(*)
    * When I return home, I'll look for a better job.
   
    
   
  - while
   
   1) while은 접속사이므로 뒤에 절이 오고 during은 전치사이므로 명사가 온다.
   
    * The Japanese have a tendency to keep silent while meetings.(*)
    * The Japanese have a tendency to keep silent during meetings.
   
   2) while절이 어떤 사건이 발생할 당시의 배경에 대해 이야기 할 때 while절의 시제는 과거 진행형으로 쓴다.
   
    * While I drove to the airport, my car broke down.(*)
    * While I was driving to the airport, my car broke down.
   
    
   
  - whole
   
   1) one the whole은 '일반적으로 말해서(generally speaking)'이라는 뜻이다. as a whole은 '전체로서, 
      하나로서'라는 뜻이다.
   
    * As a whole, I am very happy here.(*)
    * On the whole, I am very happy here.
   
    
   
  - wide
   
   1) '넓은 광경'이라고 할 때는 wide를 쓰지 않고 broad를 써서 broad view라고 한다.
   
    * My education has given me a wide view of life.(*)
    * My education has given me a broad view of life.
   
    
   
  - win
   
   1) '경기에 이기다'라고 할 때는 win과 beat를 쓸 수 있다. 그러나 win의 목적어는 game, match, race, 
      competition, election 등이 와야하고 beat의 목적어로는 person, team, group 등이 와야한다.
   
    * We have never won the American team.(*)
    * We have never beaten the American team.
   
    
   
  - worth
   
   1) worth는 주로 전치사로 쓰이며 명사로는 주로 value가 쓰인다.
   
    * The ring has great sentimental worth.(*)
    * The ring has great sentimental value.
   
   2) '가치있다'고 할 때는 be worth라고 쓴다.
   
    * The missing ring worths about two thousand pounds.(*)
    * The missing ring is worth about two thousand pounds.
   
   3) worth는 목적어로 -ing를 취한다. to부정사를 취하지 않는다.
   
    * It's also worth to visit the north of England if you have time.(*)
    * It's also worth visiting the north of England if you have time.
   
    
   
  - yearly
   
   1) yearly는 주로 일년에 한번씩 일어나는 것 혹은 일년에 생산되는 총량을 말할 때 쓴다. 반면에 every year이나 
      each year는 주로 '줄곧(all the time)'이라는 듯으로 쓰인다.
   
    * More houses are built yearly.(*)
    * More houses are built every year.
    * The interest is paid yearly.
    * The yearly catch rose to a peak of 52,000 tones.
    * We were manufacturing and selling about 20,000 tones yearly.
    * The country's tourist industry is growing ever year.
